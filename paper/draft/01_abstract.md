# Abstract

Epileptic seizure recognition from EEG is challenging because signals are noisy and non-stationary, and method comparisons are often not fully reproducible. This study introduces a deterministic Cartesian soft-computing benchmark that evaluates preprocessing, feature reduction, feature selection, and classification in one unified pipeline.

The framework evaluates two tracks (binary and multiclass) with 4 preprocessing methods, 4 reduction options, 8 selection strategies, and 6 classifiers under 3-fold stratified CV, yielding 1,536 unique pipelines and 4,608 fold evaluations. In the validated full run, all fold evaluations were processed (`4392` successful, `216` safely skipped/failed with logged reasons). The best binary pipeline (`svm + quantile + pca + none`) achieved `accuracy=0.976261`, while the best multiclass pipeline (`mlp_ann + minmax + pca + none`) achieved `accuracy=0.685651`. The output stack (metrics, ranking tables, plots, and paper drafts) provides an auditable and reproducible basis for research reporting.
